# ABSTRACT

Micro/nanofibres have been emerging as an interdisciplinary platform for exploring novel sensing technology on the micro/nanoscale. This work proposes and demonstrates an ultrasensitive and stable pressure sensor based on the perturbation of adiabatic conditions during the optical mode evolving in the transition region. In our experiment, when attached to a glass slide, the sensor shows ultrahigh pressure sensitivity of $1.52\mathrm{kPa}^{-1}$ which is about three times the sensitivity of the waist region. Simultaneously, the sensing region of the fibre has a diameter of tens of microns to provide a better mechanical structure strength. These preliminary results demonstrate that the sensor has the advantages of stable structure, high sensitivity, and simple preparation process, which may have potential in practical applications. 

# 1. Introduction

Optical fibre sensors are of wide applications in many fields, such as refractive index, temperature, humidity and pressure measurement. The typical configurations of optical fibre sensors including fibre Bragg Grating [1-3], Mach-Zehnder interferometer [4,5], Fabry-Perot interferometer [6,7], Sagnac interferometer [8,9] and so on. In 2003, Tong and Mazur demonstrated the low-loss micro/nanofibres (MNFs) with diameters far below the wavelength of the guided light [10]. From then on, MNF sensors have attracted increasing attention due to their high-sensitive response to the tiny changes of external stimuli and potential for miniaturized sensors with a small footprint, high flexibility, and high-speed responsiveness [11-15]. Very recently, MNFs were used for assembling flexible and wearable sensors which can overcome the parasitic effect, insufficient insulation, and electromagnetic disturbance in traditional electronic sensors [16]. Wearable optical sensors offer a number of highly attractive advantages, including inherent electrical safety, immunity to electromagnetic interference, and resistance to corrosion, for monitoring various mechanical stimuli and physiological signals, which are significant for future real-time health monitoring and the realization of precise medicine [17]. 

Many novel schemes have been developed to realize wearable optical sensors [16-22]. One simple and effective sensing mechanism is based on the light leakage induced by the bending of the waist region under the external pressure. Using this principle, Zhang et al. proposed a highly sensitive optical pressure sensor by embedding a straight MNF into a polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) film [16]. To improve the stretchability of the sensor in Ref. [16], Pan et al. prebend the MNF 

before being embedded into the PDMS film [19]. Despite the success of these proposed MNF sensors in terms of scalability and flexibility, their sensing regions have a diameter ranging from hundreds of nanometers to several microns and are extremely fragile, which limits the mechanical structural strength of the sensors and increases the preparation difficulty of sensors. In addition, the sensitivity of the sensors also needs to be improved. Therefore, it is a challenge for the realization of MNF sensors with high mechanical structural strength and high sensitivity simultaneously. 

In this work, we report an ultrasensitive and stable pressure sensor based on the transition region of a tapered optical fibre. The physical mechanism is based on the perturbation of adiabatic conditions when the guided mode evolves from the core mode to the cladding mode in the transition region. Different from the waist-region-based sensor in the previous papers [16,19], here, we use the transition region to achieve the sensor. In our proof-of-concept experiment, when attached to a glass slide, the sensor is proved to have an ultrahigh sensitivity of $1.52\mathrm{kPa}^{-1}$ in the transition region which is about three times the sensitivity of the waist region. The transition region has a diameter of tens of microns, which provides a stronger mechanical structure and simple preparation process. Particularly, the choice of substrate material can be abundant and various, which may endow the sensor with more sensing functions.

# 摘要

微/纳米纤维作为一种跨学科平台，正逐渐成为探索微/纳尺度新型传感技术的重要载体。本研究提出并展示了一种基于光模在过渡区域演化过程中绝热条件扰动的高灵敏度、高稳定性压力传感器。实验中，当传感器附着于载玻片时，其压力灵敏度高达 $1.52\mathrm{kPa}^{-1}$ ，约为腰部区域灵敏度的三倍。同时，该光纤传感区域的直径仅为数十微米，具备更优的机械结构强度。这些初步结果表明，该传感器具有结构稳定、灵敏度高、制备工艺简单等优势，在实际应用中展现出潜在价值。

# 1. 引言

光纤传感器在折射率、温度、湿度和压力测量等诸多领域具有广泛应用。典型的光纤传感器结构包括光纤布拉格光栅[1-3]、马赫-曾德尔干涉仪[4,5]、法布里-珀罗干涉仪[6,7]、萨格纳克干涉仪[8,9]等。2003年，To ng和Mazur展示了直径远小于导波波长的低损耗微纳光纤[10]。此后，微纳光纤传感器因其对外界刺激微小变化的高灵敏度响应，以及在微型化传感器方面具有小尺寸、高柔韧性和快速响应等潜力而受到越来越多的关注[11-15]。最近，微纳光纤被用于组装柔性可穿戴传感器，能够克服传统电子传感器中的寄生效应、绝缘不足和电磁干扰等问题[16]。可穿戴光学传感器在监测各类机械刺激与生理信号方面具备诸多显著优势，包括本质电气安全性、抗电磁干扰能力和耐腐蚀特性，这对未来实现实时健康监测与精准医疗具有重要意义[17]。

许多新颖方案已被开发用于实现可穿戴光学传感器[16-22]。一种简单有效的传感机制基于外部压力下腰部区域弯曲引起的光泄漏。利用这一原理，张等人通过将直微纳光纤嵌入聚二甲基硅氧烷薄膜中，提出了一种高灵敏度光学压力传感器[16]。为提升文献[16]中传感器的可拉伸性，潘等人对微纳光纤进行了预弯曲处理。

在嵌入PDMS薄膜之前[19]。尽管这些提出的MNF传感器在可扩展性和灵活性方面取得了成功，但其传感区域的直径范围从数百纳米到数微米，且极其脆弱，这限制了传感器的机械结构强度，并增加了传感器的制备难度。此外，传感器的灵敏度也有待提高。因此，如何同时实现具有高机械结构强度和高灵敏度的MNF传感器仍是一个挑战。

在本工作中，我们报道了一种基于锥形光纤过渡区的超灵敏、高稳定性压力传感器。其物理机制基于过渡区中导模从纤芯模演化至包层模时绝热条件受扰动而产生的响应。与先前文献[16,19]中基于腰区的传感器不同，本研究利用过渡区实现传感功能。在概念验证实验中，当传感器附着于玻璃基片时，其过渡区表现出 $1.52\mathrm{kPa}^{-1}$ 的超高灵敏度，约为腰区灵敏度的三倍。过渡区直径仅为数十微米，具备更强的机械结构且制备工艺简单。特别值得注意的是，衬底材料的选择范围广泛多样，这有望赋予传感器更丰富的传感功能。

The physical mechanism of the sensor is shown in Fig. 1(f). The transition region profile everywhere along the taper consists of a core embedded in a finite cladding, which is surrounded by air. When the fundamental mode $\mathrm{LP}_{01}$ propagates from the original fibre through the transition region to the uniform waist region, it has a loss of power because of the departure from the translational invariance of the uniform fibre [26]. The power loss can be described accurately through coupling to the finite set of discrete cladding modes propagating in the cladding. If the local taper angle $\Omega(z)$ is small enough everywhere in the transition region the fundamental mode can be considered unperturbed as it evolves from being core-guided to cladding-guided, and it propagates approximately with negligible loss [27]. Conversely, if $\Omega(z)$ is large enough, the fundamental mode couples to the higher-order cladding modes with the nearest propagation constant. The critical 

taper angle $\Omega$ is defined as $\Omega = a(\beta_{f} - \beta_{cl}) / 2\pi$ , $a$ is the core radius, $\beta_{f}$ is the propagation constant of the fundamental mode and $\beta_{cl}$ is the propagation constant of the cladding mode. In our experiment, the angle of the transition region is controlled to be slightly smaller than the critical taper angle. Thus, when the fibre is under pressure, the local bending in the transition region will cause the angle on one side of fibre to exceed the critical angle, and perturb the adiabaticity. This will couple the fundamental mode into the higher-order cladding modes [28,29]. For the waist of a tapered fibre, the light is guided by the cladding/air interface. The difference in refractive index is significant, in this case, the degenerate LP modes separate into the constituent transverse electric (TE), transverse magnetic (TM) and hybrid (odd and even (HE) and (EH)) modes. In our experiment, the waist region only supports the $\mathrm{HE}_{11}$ mode, the higher-order cladding modes will be filtered out at the waist region and cause significant loss. In the transition region, the mode guidance changes from core-guided to cladding-guided, and the coupling between the fundamental mode and higher-order cladding mode through this region is very sensitive to the taper angle, thus achieving ultrasensitivity.

Additionally, the thickness of the top layer PDMS film can affect the response threshold and sensitivity of the sensor. Hence, we measured the pressure response of the sensor with different top layer PDMS film thicknesses (approximately 130, 260, 390, and $520~\mu \mathrm{m}$ respectively). From Fig. 3(a) we can see that as the thickness of the top layer PDMS film increases, the sensor has a larger response threshold and smaller sensitivity. Fig. 3(b) shows the measured thresholds for different thicknesses, the values are 0.74, 1.32, 4.20, and $9.35\mathrm{kPa}$ , respectively. This can be easily understood that the thinner the PDMS film, the easier it is to convert mechanical pressure into fibre deformation. Thus, the thinner the PDMS film will result in a smaller response threshold and higher sensitivity. For our sensor, there is no evanescent field in the transition regions, where the top PDMS layer is used to fix the sensor. Therefore, the top layer PDMS film can be extremely thin and may indicate a higher sensitivity. 

To exclude the influence of the waist region on the ultrahigh sensitivity observed in our experiment, we prepared four sensor samples with different pull lengths. All four tapered fibres have the same exponential decay profile, thus, they have the same transition regions. sensitivity almost have no change, so we can exclude the influence of the waist region on the pressure sensitivity. Therefore, when the tapered optical fibre is used as pressure sensing, the waist diameter of the fibre does not need to be very small, which can greatly reduce the preparation difficulty of sensors, and provide better mechanical structure strength. 

To further study the relationship between the sensitivity and taper angle, we prepared six linear profile fibre sensor samples with different taper angles. The angle is controlled by the transition region length for the linear profile fibre. Fig. 3(d) shows the experimental results of the six samples with transition region lengths of 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 and $30\mathrm{mm}$ , respectively. It is obvious that when the length is $25\mathrm{mm}$ (corresponding to an angle of $0.17^{\circ}$ ), the maximum pressure sensitivity of about $0.94\mathrm{kPa}^{-1}$ is obtained. This is because when the taper angle is far smaller than the critical taper angle $\Omega$ , the pressure needs to be large enough to perturb the adiabatic conditions. For taper angles larger than $\Omega$ , failure of the adiabatic conditions has resulted in significant loss, therefore, the sensitivity becomes low. Here, the difference in sensitivity is due to the errors in the manual preparation process of the sensors. 

In Fig. 3(e), we plotted the sensitivity as a function of the taper angle for both the exponential and the linear profile fibres. It clearly shows that the optimal sensitivity of exponential and linear profile fibres are both at a taper angle of $0.17^{\circ}$ . The result also indicates the self-consistency of our scheme. In addition, Fig. 3(f) shows the measured pressure sensitivity for ten cycles. The result indicates that the sensor has a sensing repeat error rate of approximately $1\%$ , which shows good stability, repeatability, and excellent reliability. 

Comparing to the schemes in Ref. [16,19], our scheme uses the transition region for sensing, which has a larger diameter. The large diameter makes the sensor more robust to external disturbance and has better mechanical structure strength. More importantly, the sensitivity of the sensors are also increased. In the future, similar to Ref. [16], we can operate the U-shaped sensor in a suspension mode, which may result in higher sensitivity and flexibility than in a rigid substrate.

此外，顶层PDMS薄膜的厚度会影响传感器的响应阈值和灵敏度。因此，我们测量了传感器在不同顶层PDMS薄膜厚度（分别约为130、260、390和 $520\mu \mathrm{m}$ ）下的压力响应。从图3(a)可以看出，随着顶层PDMS薄膜厚度的增加，传感器的响应阈值增大，灵敏度降低。图3(b)展示了不同厚度对应的测量阈值，分别为0.74、1.32、4.20和 $9.35\mathrm{kPa}$ 。这很容易理解：PDMS薄膜越薄，机械压力越容易转化为光纤形变。因此，PDMS薄膜越薄，响应阈值越小，灵敏度越高。对于我们的传感器，在过渡区域（即顶层PDMS层用于固定传感器的部分）不存在倏逝场。因此，顶层PDMS薄膜可以做得极薄，这可能意味着更高的灵敏度。

为了进一步研究灵敏度与锥角之间的关系，我们制备了六个具有不同锥角的线性轮廓光纤传感器样品。锥角通过线性轮廓光纤的过渡区长度进行控制。图3(d)展示了过渡区长度分别为5、10、15、20、25和30毫米的六个样品的实验结果。显然，当长度为25毫米（对应角度为 $0.17^{\circ}$ ）时，获得了约 $0.94\mathrm{kPa}^{-1}$ 的最大压力灵敏度。这是因为当锥角远小于临界锥角 $\Omega$ 时，需要足够大的压力才能扰动绝热条件。对于大于 $\Omega$ 的锥角，绝热条件的失效导致了显著损耗，因此灵敏度变低。此处灵敏度的差异源于传感器手工制备过程中的误差。

在图3(e)中，我们绘制了指数型和线性剖面光纤的灵敏度随锥角变化的函数关系。结果清晰表明，指数型与线性剖面光纤的最优灵敏度均出现在锥角为 $0.17^{\circ}$ 处。这一结果也验证了我们方案的自治性。此外，图3(f)展示了十个循环周期内测得的压力灵敏度数据。结果表明该传感器的感知重复误差率约为 $1\%$ ，展现出良好的稳定性、可重复性及优异的可靠性。
