# ABSTRACT

This study presents the detection of alcohol concentrations through the development of a twisted tapered plastic optical fiber (POF) sensor. The sensor is fabricated through a simple heating and drawing technique. Then, the tapered POF is twisted together to create a sophisticated coupled system that functions as a sensor. The sensing principle of the sensor is fundamentally based on the intensity variation in the coupled optical power. The structure consists of two input ports and two output ports where the light is launched in the first fiber (illuminating fiber), and the measurement of coupled power is taken from the receiving fiber. The sensor successfully detects ethanol, propanol, butanol, and pentanol with a comprehensive analysis of parameters across different tapered fibers. Significantly, sensor efficacy improves when the illuminating fiber's diameter is smaller than that of the receiving fiber. Achieving a sensitivity of $506\%/\mathrm{RIU}$ , the optimal configuration includes an illuminating fiber diameter of $200\mu \mathrm{m}$ , a receiving fiber diameter of $400\mu \mathrm{m}$ , and an $8\mathrm{mm}$ twisted region. The temperature effect is also analyzed up to $50^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . This POF-based alcohol sensor is economical and user-friendly, featuring simple fabrication and operation. Its sensitivity, cost-effectiveness, and ease of use make it a promising tool for alcohol measurement applications, representing a significant advancement in optical sensing technologies.

# 摘要

本研究通过开发一种扭曲锥形塑料光纤（POF）传感器，实现了对酒精浓度的检测。该传感器采用简单的加热拉伸技术制备而成。随后，将锥形POF进行扭曲，形成一个精密的耦合系统，作为传感器使用。该传感器的传感原理主要基于耦合光功率的强度变化。其结构包含两个输入端口和两个输出端口，光线首先注入第一根光纤（照明光纤），耦合功率的测量则从接收光纤获取。该传感器成功检测了乙醇、丙醇、丁醇和戊醇，并对不同锥形光纤的参数进行了全面分析。值得注意的是，当照明光纤的直径小于接收光纤时，传感器的效能会得到提升。在最佳配置下，即照明光纤直径为 $200\mu$ 微米、接收光纤直径为 $400\mu$ 微米、扭曲区域长度为8毫米时，灵敏度达到了 $506\%$ RIU。此外，研究还分析了高达 $50^{\circ}$ 摄氏度的温度效应。这种基于POF的酒精传感器经济实用、操作简便，具有制造和使用简单的特点。其高灵敏度、成本效益和易用性使其成为酒精测量应用中极具前景的工具，代表了光学传感技术的重要进展。

# 1. 引言

对于一种选择性、快速且可靠的酒精测量技术的检测，在众多领域都至关重要，涵盖食品加工、临床实践、医学研究以及环境和农业评估等行业。然而，确保食品和医疗产品的安全与优质仍是一项复杂的挑战，这需要传感器技术不断演进，具备更强大的功能。

Diverse analytical techniques for alcohol detection have been employed to evaluate various analytes within intricate matrices, encompassing chromatography [9,10], immunochromatography [10], mass spectrometry [11], nuclear magnetic resonance [12], polymerase chain reaction, ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy [13], Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) [14], infrared spectroscopy [15], surface-enhanced Raman spectroscopy (SERS) [16], Raman spectroscopy [17], circular dichroism spectroscopy, spectrofluorimetry, among others, which retain relevance [18]. Nonetheless, these methodologies have limitations, with some marred by inadequate sensitivity and specificity. Challenges pertaining to portability, slow response times, and intricate operational procedures further accentuate the need for innovative analytical approaches that afford rapid, targeted analysis and straightforward interpretation of results. The development of optical-fiber sensors to replace conventional methods constitutes a possible strategy to address these hurdles [19,20]. Optical sensors offer a multitude of advantages, among them being their high sensitivity and selectivity, which permit precise detection and measurement. Their non-invasive nature preserves sample integrity, eliminating contamination risks. Additionally, these sensors are renowned for their resilience against electromagnetic interference, ensuring accurate performance in challenging environments and boasting durability with minimal upkeep requirements, making them a reliable choice across industries [21]. Based on these advantages, optical fiber demonstrates great potential in chemical and biological sensing domains. 

Optical sensing methodologies have long been recognized as effective means for alcohol detection. More than thirty years back, fiber optic near-infrared spectroscopy was instrumented in alcohol quantification [22]. Since then, sophisticated and advanced methods have been introduced to enhance sensitivity. A notable innovation entailed the fusion of fiber optics with multi-walled carbon nanotubes/ $\mathrm{Co}_{3}\mathrm{O}_{4}$ , demonstrating high sensitivity levels—35 counts per ppm for ethanol and 51 counts per ppm for ammonia—with excellent discrimination in gaseous samples [23]. Another noteworthy alcohol sensor design involved an SPR-based glass rod sensor, its surface adorned with gold and an $\alpha$ -mercaptoethyl- $\omega$ -methoxy polyoxyethylene (PEG thiol) layer, topped with Teflon AF2400. This sensor excelled in selectively sensing water and ethanol [24]. Fluorescence activation through terphenyl-ol derivatives furnishes another instance of advanced optical sensors. The terphenyl-ol and sodium carbonate blend selectively reacts to ethanol, emitting sky-blue light, and its integration with a water-absorbent polymer further refines ethanol detection limits [24]. Si quantum dot (QD)-embedded optical fibers represent yet another breakthrough, capable of identifying ethanol and water within $15\mathrm{s}$ through shifts in the QDs' fluorescence intensity under blue light exposure [25]. The sensor's response mechanism relied on alterations in the fluorescence intensity emitted by Si-quantum dots, which undergo an elevation upon exposure to blue light illumination in the presence of alcohol and water vapor. These examples illustrate the progression and promise of optical sensing systems in alcohol detection while these methods are effective in certain scenarios, they still face some limitations, such as sensitivity to environmental conditions, apply appropriate coating and complexity in implementation. 

In another optical design, the alcohol sensor was designed using lipophilic Reichardt's dyes in polymer membranes but has limitations due to the pH value effect in the solution [26]. Penza et al. integrated a single-walled carbon nanotube into silica optical fiber and quartz crystal microbalance to demonstrate an alcohol sensor [27]. The optical microfiber sensor was also used for alcohol detection in a study [28]. A few alcohol sensors have also been reported based on changes in refractive index [29-31]. Morisawa et al. [31] reported a polymer optical fiber-based alcohol sensor where a change in RI was caused by Nonolac resin that swelled when exposed to alcohol. A surface plasmon resonance (SPR)-based optical fiber sensor system, incorporating a gold layer, was devised without necessitating a rotating stage or spectrometer. This system was subsequently validated for its capability to conduct 

detailed quantitative assessments of a range of alcohol types [30]. A demonstration showcased a tapered fiber optic sensor proficient in identifying aliphatic alcohols, including methanol, ethanol, 1-propanol, and 2-propanol [31]. The sensor operates by detecting alterations in the refractive index within the evanescent field of the fiber's tapered zone, induced by the presence of these alcohols, which in turn modifies the output power. 

In this study, we propose a novel alcohol detection sensor based on a twisted tapered polymer optical fiber (POF) structure. The sensing mechanism is driven by changes in refractive index (RI), which occur due to variations in the coupling medium that varies the coupled intensity. The sensor comprises two twisted POFs, tapered through a controlled heating and drawing process. To evaluate the sensor's performance, we fabricated multiple tapered fibers and analyzed their sensitivity in detecting different alcohol variants. The sensor effectively detects ethanol, propanol, butanol, and pentanol with a thorough analysis of key parameters across various tapered fiber configurations. Notably, the sensor's performance is enhanced when the diameter of the illuminating fiber is smaller than that of the receiving fiber. The optimal configuration, achieving a sensitivity of $506\%/\mathrm{RIU}$ , includes an illuminating fiber diameter of $200\mu \mathrm{m}$ , a receiving fiber diameter of $400\mu \mathrm{m}$ , and an $8\mathrm{mm}$ twisted region. Detailed explanations of the sensor fabrication process and its working principle are presented in the methodology section, while the experimental results and corresponding discussion are provided in the results section.

A step-index fiber's refractive index (RI) changes abruptly at the boundary between the core and cladding. The core (which is the central light-guiding part of the fiber) is made of polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), a common transparent plastic. One crucial aspect of tapering the fiber is its effect on the evanescent field, which is the portion of the light field that extends beyond the physical boundary of the fiber. # 2.2. Sensing principle

When a POF undergoes a tapering process, its geometry changes, causing a redistribution of the light traveling within the fiber. Ordinarily, light in a POF propagates primarily in the core through what is called the fundamental mode. However, tapering the fiber—reducing its diameter gradually along its length— affects the way light travels through it. In a tapered POF, the confinement of light becomes less strict due to the altered geometry. Higher-order modes, which are light paths that would normally not propagate effectively in a non-tapered fiber due to their spatial distribution, start to play a more significant role. These higher-order modes cannot be fully contained within the narrowed core and thus begin to migrate into the cladding layer. As a result, two types of modes are generated from this migration. 

Radiation Modes are modes that do not remain confined within the fiber structure and instead leak or radiate out into the surrounding environment. They do not contribute to the guided transmission of light along the fiber. Unlike radiation modes, cladding modes are still partially trapped by the fiber [37-39]. They bounce back and forth between the core-cladding interface due to total internal reflection, a phenomenon in which light reflects within a medium with a higher refractive index when it encounters a boundary with a lower refractive index. Despite being confined, these modes have an evanescent field, which is an oscillatory field that decays exponentially away from the fiber surface into the external medium. 

The evanescent field associated with cladding modes penetrates the external medium adjacent to the fiber. This field, though rapidly decreasing in intensity, can interact with the medium, making it sensitive to changes in the refractive index of that medium. The penetration depth $d_{p}$ of this evanescent field is a measure of how far into the external medium this field extends before its intensity drops to a negligible level. It's mathematically described by a formula that typically takes into account the refractive indices of the core, cladding, and the external medium, as well as the wavelength of the light. This depth is crucial in understanding and designing sensors based on tapered fibers, as it directly influences their sensitivity to environmental changes. The penetration depth $d_{p}$ can be mathematically expressed as [40]: 

$$
d _ {p} = \frac {\lambda}{2 \pi \sqrt {n _ {c l a d} ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta - n _ {e x t e r m a l} ^ {2}}} \tag {1}
$$

Where $\lambda$ is the wavelength of the light in a vacuum, $n_{\text{clad}}$ is the refractive index of the cladding, $n_{\text{external}}$ is the refractive index of the external medium, and $\theta$ is the incident angle. # 2.2. Sensing principle

当POF经历锥形化过程时，其几何形状发生变化，导致光纤内传输的光重新分布。通常，POF中的光主要通过所谓的基本模式在纤芯中传播。然而，将光纤锥形化——即沿其长度逐渐减小直径——会影响光在其中的传播方式。在锥形POF中，由于几何形状的改变，光的限制变得不那么严格。高阶模式（即因其空间分布通常在非锥形光纤中无法有效传播的光路）开始发挥更重要的作用。这些高阶模式无法完全被限制在变窄的纤芯内，因此开始迁移到包层中。结果，这种迁移产生了两种类型的模式。

辐射模式是指那些不局限于光纤结构内部，而是泄漏或辐射到周围环境中的模式。它们对光沿光纤的导波传输没有贡献。与辐射模式不同，包层模式仍部分受光纤束缚[37-39]。由于全内反射现象——即光在较高折射率介质中传播时遇到较低折射率介质边界会发生反射——这些模式在纤芯-包层界面间反复反射。尽管受到束缚，这些模式仍具有倏逝场，这是一种从光纤表面向外部介质呈指数衰减的振荡场。

与包层模相关的倏逝场会渗透到光纤相邻的外部介质中。该场虽然强度迅速衰减，但仍能与介质相互作用，从而对介质折射率的变化保持敏感。该倏逝场的穿透深度 $d_{p}$ 是衡量该场在强度降至可忽略水平前延伸至外部介质多远的指标。其数学描述通常考虑纤芯、包层和外部介质的折射率以及光波长。该深度对于理解和设计基于锥形光纤的传感器至关重要，因为它直接影响传感器对环境变化的灵敏度。穿透深度 $d_{p}$ 可通过数学公式表达为[40]：

In the context of optical fiber sensors, especially those utilizing twisted structures, the interaction between the propagating light and the surrounding medium is critical for sensing purposes. When the twisted region is longer, there is a greater length of interaction between the evanescent field (an area of light intensity that extends slightly beyond the physical boundaries of the fiber) and the external medium whose RI is being sensed. This extended interaction enhances the sensitivity of the sensor to subtle variations in the RI because more light is affected by the changes in the external environment. Consequently, even minor alterations in the refractive properties of the surrounding medium can lead to more pronounced changes in the sensor's output signal, improving its overall sensitivity and accuracy in detecting RI variations. 

The study also examined how changes in temperature impact the performance of the sensor, with the results displayed in Fig. 8. For this test, the sensor was configured with an illuminating fiber diameter of $200\mu \mathrm{m}$ and a receiving fiber diameter of $300\mu \mathrm{m}$ , and the length of the twisted region was maintained at $8\mathrm{mm}$ . To assess the temperature effect, the twisted portion of the fiber was submerged in liquid, and the temperature of the water varied systematically from 20 to $50^{\circ}$ Celsius. Findings revealed that the sensor's sensitivity to temperature alterations was approximately 0.002 per degree Celsius. This sensitivity arises from a combination of two primary effects: (1) thermo-optic effect and (2) thermal expansion effect. 

Thermo-optic effect refers to the change in the refractive index of the fiber material due to temperature variations. As temperature increases, the refractive index of the fiber typically changes, influencing the light propagation characteristics and thus affecting the coupling efficiency and output of the sensor. Temperature fluctuations can also cause physical expansion or contraction of the fiber material due to the thermal expansion effect. In the case of the twisted tapered POFs, such changes can lead to alterations in the fiber's geometry, including the diameter, pitch of the twist, or the distance between the fibers, which in turn can impact the light guidance and coupling properties, contributing to the observed temperature sensitivity. Meanwhile, the detected temperature sensitivity was relatively low, indicating a stable performance of the sensor over a wide temperature range. These findings emphasize the importance of considering temperature effects in the interpretation of sensor data and potentially necessitating temperature compensation strategies in certain applications.

# 4. Conclusions

The development and evaluation of a twisted tapered plastic optical fiber alcohol sensor have demonstrated its remarkable potential for accurate and sensitive detection of alcohol concentrations in liquid media. Through a straightforward heating and drawing technique, these tapered POFs were fabricated and twisted, creating an intricate yet highly functional coupled system. Four types of alcohol variants (ethanol, propanol, butanol, and pentanol) were detected. Meanwhile, the sensor's parameters were also analyzed with different tapered fibers. Notably, the sensor's efficacy significantly improved when the illuminating fiber's diameter was lesser than its receiving fiber. The sensor sensitivity of 506/RIU was achieved with an illuminating fiber diameter of $200\mu \mathrm{m}$ , a receiving fiber diameter of $400~{\mu\mathrm{m}}$ and a substantial twisted region of about $8\mathrm{mm}$ . This twisted tapered POF-based alcohol sensor embodies an economical and user-friendly diagnostic tool characterized by facile fabrication processes and straightforward operational mechanics. Its unique blend of sensitivity, cost-effectiveness, and ease of use positions it as a highly favorable candidate for alcohol measurement applications, thereby offering a pivotal advancement in the realm of optical sensing technologies. In the future, the study can further elaborate on a multiplexing technique that integrates multiple sensors on a single optical fiber.

# 4.结论

一种扭曲锥形塑料光纤酒精传感器的开发与评估，已证明其在液体介质中精确灵敏检测酒精浓度方面具有显著潜力。通过简单的加热拉伸技术，这些锥形塑料光纤被制造并扭曲，形成了一个复杂但功能强大的耦合系统。该传感器成功检测了四种酒精变体（乙醇、丙醇、丁醇和戊醇）。同时，还分析了传感器在不同锥形光纤下的参数。值得注意的是，当照明光纤的直径小于接收光纤时，传感器的效能显著提高。在照明光纤直径为200微米、接收光纤直径为400微米、扭曲区域长度约为8毫米的条件下，传感器灵敏度达到了506/RIU。这种基于扭曲锥形塑料光纤的酒精传感器，以其制造工艺简便、操作机制直观的特点，体现了一种经济且用户友好的诊断工具。其独特的灵敏度、成本效益和易用性相结合，使其成为酒精测量应用中极具优势的候选方案，从而为光学传感技术领域提供了关键进展。未来，研究可进一步阐述在单根光纤上集成多个传感器的复用技术。
